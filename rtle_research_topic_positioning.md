# Research Topic Positioning: Academic Performance, Pre-Board Exams, and RTLE Success

## Recommended Positioning

This study should be positioned as a **defensible, institution-level predictive validation study**, not as a broad study on what *causes* Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination (RTLE) success.

The best framing is:

> **An institution-level predictive validation study examining how undergraduate academic performance, course-cluster performance, and pre-board examination results are associated with and predict Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination performance.**

This positioning fits the available data:

- Student grades by batch
- Student grades by cluster
- Pre-board grades
- Board exam grades

## Recommended Working Title

**Academic Performance, Pre-Board Examination Results, and Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination Performance: An Institution-Level Predictive Study**

Alternative, more technical title:

**Predictive Validity of Course-Cluster Grades and Pre-Board Examination Results for Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination Performance**

The second title is stronger if the study wants to emphasize validation and practical institutional use.

## Core Research Framing

The study should be framed around **predictive validity** and **institutional decision-making**.

The central idea:

> The school already collects academic and pre-board data, but it is unclear which indicators meaningfully predict actual RTLE performance. This study validates whether these internal measures can be used for early identification, targeted remediation, and review planning.

This avoids overclaiming. The study should not claim that grades or pre-board exams *cause* board success. Instead, it should claim that they may serve as **screening, forecasting, and remediation-planning indicators**.

## Available Data and Best Use

| Available Data | Best Use |
|---|---|
| Student grades by batch | Cohort/batch trends; possible batch effects |
| Student grades by cluster | Subject-area or course-domain predictors |
| Pre-board grades | Readiness indicator; likely strong predictor |
| Board exam grades | Main outcome; may be continuous or converted to pass/fail |

## Recommended Problem Statement

Radiologic Technology programs routinely monitor student academic performance and administer pre-board examinations to assess board readiness. However, the extent to which these institutional indicators predict actual Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination performance remains insufficiently validated in local program contexts. This study examines the relationship between course-cluster grades, pre-board examination results, and RTLE grades among graduates of the program to determine which academic indicators most strongly predict licensure examination performance and may be used to guide targeted remediation and review planning.

## Suggested Scope Boundary

### Include

- Academic grades
- Clustered course grades
- Pre-board grades
- Board exam grades
- Batch/cohort differences
- Predictive modeling

### Avoid or Limit

Avoid including these unless data are actually available:

- Full curriculum evaluation
- Teaching effectiveness
- Student motivation
- Review center effects
- Mental health
- Faculty quality
- Clinical skills
- Socioeconomic factors

The study should focus on predictors of licensure success. It should not attempt to evaluate the entire curriculum, broadly compare institutions, or infer causality.

## Strongest Possible Contribution

### 1. Local Validation

The study validates whether the institution’s own academic indicators actually predict RTLE performance.

This matters because even if other studies found pre-board exams useful, each school’s pre-board exam may differ in difficulty, alignment, and quality.

### 2. Course-Cluster Analysis

This is one of the strongest assets of the available data.

Many studies only use overall GWA. If grades by cluster are available, the study can ask:

> Which academic clusters are most associated with RTLE performance?

This makes the study more useful than a simple “GWA vs board rating” paper.

Possible cluster-level insights may include whether clusters related to imaging, radiation physics, anatomy/positioning, professional courses, or general education are stronger predictors.

### 3. Practical Remediation Model

The end product can be a practical institutional framework:

> Students with weak performance in specific clusters and low pre-board scores may be flagged for targeted review before the RTLE.

This makes the study useful for program planning and student support.

## Recommended Research Questions

### Main Research Question

**To what extent do undergraduate academic performance, course-cluster grades, and pre-board examination results predict Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination performance?**

### Specific Research Questions

1. What is the profile of students’ academic grades, course-cluster grades, pre-board grades, and RTLE grades by batch?
2. Is there a significant relationship between overall academic performance and RTLE grades?
3. Is there a significant relationship between course-cluster grades and RTLE grades?
4. Is there a significant relationship between pre-board grades and RTLE grades?
5. Which predictor or combination of predictors best explains variation in RTLE grades?
6. Do pre-board grades add predictive value beyond academic grades and course-cluster grades?

The sixth question is especially important because it tests whether the pre-board exam contributes additional predictive value beyond regular academic performance.

## Recommended Statistical Positioning

Since board exam grades are available, the primary analysis should treat RTLE rating as a **continuous outcome**.

### Primary Outcome

**RTLE board exam grade/rating**

### Secondary Outcome

If useful, the board result may also be converted into a binary outcome:

| Category | Coding |
|---|---|
| Passed | 1 |
| Failed | 0 |

### Suggested Analyses

| Outcome or Purpose | Suggested Analysis |
|---|---|
| Board exam grade/rating | Pearson or Spearman correlation; multiple linear regression |
| Pass/fail status | Logistic regression |
| Cluster-level predictors | Regression model using cluster grades |
| Batch differences | ANOVA, Kruskal-Wallis, or batch as a control variable |
| Prediction accuracy | Cross-validation, if sample size allows |

## Recommended Conceptual Model

The simplest model is:

> **Academic preparation → Pre-board readiness → RTLE performance**

Or more formally:

| Variable Group | Role |
|---|---|
| Batch/cohort | Context or control variable |
| Course-cluster grades | Domain-specific academic predictors |
| Overall academic average/GWA | General academic predictor |
| Pre-board grade | Near-board readiness predictor |
| RTLE grade | Main outcome |

Interpretation:

> Course-cluster grades and overall academic performance represent accumulated academic preparation, while pre-board examination results represent immediate board readiness. Together, these indicators may predict RTLE performance.

## Recommended Manuscript Framing

### Introduction

Start with the importance of RTLE performance for professional entry and institutional quality assurance.

Then narrow the problem:

> Schools already collect grades and pre-board results, but the predictive value of these indicators is not always validated locally.

### Review of Related Literature

Organize the RRL around these themes:

1. Academic performance as a predictor of licensure performance
2. Course/professional-subject grades as domain-specific predictors
3. Pre-board/mock exams as readiness indicators
4. Need for local validation and psychometric caution
5. Use of predictive models for remediation and academic support

### Methods

Describe the study as:

> A retrospective descriptive-correlational and predictive study using institutional academic records, pre-board examination results, and RTLE ratings.

## Claims to Avoid

| Avoid | Better Phrasing |
|---|---|
| Grades cause board success. | Grades are associated with RTLE performance. |
| Pre-board exams determine whether students pass. | Pre-board results may serve as readiness indicators. |
| This applies to all RT schools. | Findings are institution-specific and may inform similar programs. |
| The model can guarantee passing. | The model may help identify students needing support. |
| Low pre-board score means the student will fail. | Low pre-board score may indicate elevated risk. |

## Recommended Final Positioning Statement

This study is an institution-level predictive validation of academic and pre-board indicators of RTLE performance. It contributes by identifying which routinely collected school data—overall grades, course-cluster grades, and pre-board results—are most useful for forecasting board examination performance and guiding targeted remediation.

## Best Final Title Recommendation

**Predictive Validity of Academic Performance, Course-Cluster Grades, and Pre-Board Examination Results for Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination Performance**

## Bottom Line

The topic is viable, relevant, and well-positioned if framed as a **local predictive validity study**.

The strongest value is not merely proving that “good students pass boards,” but identifying **which internal academic indicators are most useful for early warning, targeted review, and program-level decision-making**.
