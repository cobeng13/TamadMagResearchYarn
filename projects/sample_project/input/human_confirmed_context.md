### HI-0013

Question: What claims should writers avoid?

The manuscript should avoid making causal claims. The study should not state that clustered academic grades or pre-board grades cause students to pass or fail the RTLE. Instead, the study should use terms such as “associated with,” “related to,” “predictive of,” or “explained variation in.” The manuscript should also avoid claiming that the model can guarantee licensure success, replace faculty judgment, or serve as the sole basis for allowing or disallowing students to take the board examination. Claims should also not be generalized to all Radiologic Technology schools or all RTLE examinees unless the sample and design support such generalization.



Avoid these phrases:



“Pre-board grades determine RTLE success.”

“Academic grades cause board exam performance.”

“This model guarantees who will pass.”

“The findings apply to all Radiologic Technology programs.”

“Pre-board examination is the best predictor nationally.”

“Students below a certain score should not be allowed to take the board,” unless framed carefully as an institutional recommendation requiring further validation.



Safer phrasing:



“Pre-board grades were significantly associated with RTLE performance.”

“Clustered academic grades significantly predicted variation in board examination grades.”

“The model may help identify students who may benefit from additional academic support.”

“Findings should be interpreted within the context of the institution and dataset.”
