# Writing Scope

## Manuscript Sections To Prepare Later
- Introduction
- Review of Related Literature
- Methodology
- Results
- Discussion
- Conclusion
- Recommendations

## Introduction Scope
The introduction should establish the importance of licensure examination success in Radiologic Technology education, describe the need to validate institution-level academic and assessment-based predictors, and explain why clustered academic grades and pre-board grades per cluster are plausible readiness indicators. The introduction should frame the study as predictive validation for early warning, targeted remediation, review planning, and across-batch monitoring, not as a causal explanation of RTLE success. It should end with the study objectives.

## Review of Related Literature Scope
The Review of Related Literature should synthesize evidence on:
- Radiologic Technology licensure examination performance.
- Clustered academic grades and licensure outcomes.
- Professional course clusters, curriculum clusters, and board examination readiness.
- Internship or clinical performance as a competency indicator.
- Pre-board or mock board examinations as cluster-level readiness measures.
- Terminal competency or comprehensive examinations.
- Predictive models in health professions licensure studies.
- Philippine allied health licensure studies as supporting literature when Radiologic Technology-specific studies are limited.
- Local validation, psychometric caution, across-batch comparison, and institutional use of routinely collected academic and pre-board data.

## Methodology Scope
The methodology should describe:
- Retrospective descriptive-correlational and predictive design.
- Study population and sampling approach.
- Inclusion and exclusion criteria.
- Data sources and variables, including clustered academic grades, pre-board grades per cluster, RTLE board examination grades per cluster, pass/fail status, and batch/cohort.
- Data management and confidentiality procedures.
- Statistical treatment, including descriptive statistics, correlation analysis, and predictive analysis.
- Planned handling of RTLE board examination grades per cluster as performance outcomes, with pass/fail coding as the licensure success outcome.
- Planned testing of whether pre-board grades per cluster add predictive value beyond clustered academic grades, if sample size and data quality allow.
- Planned comparison of academic performance, pre-board performance, RTLE performance, and licensure success across batches.
- Ethical considerations for use of student academic records and licensure data.

## Results Scope
The Results section should present only actual findings from the available dataset. Do not invent numerical results. If results are not yet available, mark tables and values as To be confirmed. Suggested results organization:
- Participant profile.
- Academic performance profile based on clustered grades.
- Pre-board examination performance per cluster.
- RTLE outcomes in terms of board examination grades per cluster and pass/fail result.
- Correlation results.
- Predictive model results.
- Incremental predictive-value results for pre-board grades per cluster, if tested.
- Across-batch comparisons.

## Discussion Scope
The Discussion should interpret findings in relation to Radiologic Technology education and comparable allied health literature. It should explain whether clustered academic grades and pre-board grades per cluster appear useful for identifying RTLE readiness, licensure success, remediation needs, review program planning, or across-batch monitoring. Claims should remain limited to association and prediction unless causal evidence is available.

## Conclusion Scope
The conclusion should answer the updated study objectives directly and cautiously. It should identify which clustered academic or pre-board predictor set was useful, if supported by actual analysis, and note limitations of retrospective institutional data.

## Recommendations Scope
Recommendations may address:
- Early identification of at-risk students.
- Strengthening pre-board examination alignment with licensure competencies.
- Use of clustered academic grades and pre-board grades per cluster for advising and remediation.
- Improvement of terminal competency assessments.
- Future research using larger, multi-institutional samples.

## Out of Scope
- Writing the full manuscript at this stage.
- Inventing statistical values or findings.
- Making causal claims from correlational data.
- Broad institutional comparison unless the dataset includes multiple institutions.
- Evaluating faculty performance or administrative quality unless supported by data and research approval.
- Claiming that any predictor guarantees passing or failure.
- Claiming institution-specific predictive findings apply to all Radiologic Technology programs without replication.
