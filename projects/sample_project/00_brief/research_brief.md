# Research Brief

## Working Title
Predictive Value of Clustered Academic Grades and Pre-Board Cluster Grades for Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination Performance and Licensure Success Across Batches

## Brief Study Description
This study will examine whether clustered academic grades and pre-board grades per cluster are associated with and predict Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination outcomes among Bachelor of Science in Radiologic Technology graduates in the Philippines. The study will use a retrospective descriptive-correlational and predictive design based on available academic records, pre-board examination results, board examination grades per cluster, and pass/fail licensure outcomes across batches. The study should be framed as an institution-level predictive validation study, not as a broad causal study of what determines RTLE success.

## Research Problem
Radiologic Technology graduates must pass the Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination to become registered practitioners. However, licensure performance varies across graduates, board examination clusters, and batches. Programs need evidence-based indicators that can identify students who may need academic support before taking the licensure examination. Clustered academic grades and pre-board grades per cluster may help predict RTLE performance and licensure success, but their relative, incremental, and batch-level predictive value in the local institutional context remains a narrow and researchable issue requiring further review and analysis.

## Context and Rationale
The study is situated in Philippine Radiologic Technology education and licensure preparation. Prior literature in health professions education suggests that academic performance, professional course grades, clinical performance, admission indicators, comprehensive examinations, and mock board examinations may be associated with licensure outcomes. For the current study, literature should be interpreted through the specific focus on clustered academic grades, pre-board grades per cluster, RTLE board examination grades per cluster, pass/fail licensure success, and batch comparisons. Radiologic Technology-specific literature should be prioritized. Allied health studies in Nursing, Medical Technology, Dentistry, Pharmacy, and related programs should be used only as supporting evidence where Radiologic Technology literature is limited.

## General Objective
To determine the relationship and predictive value of clustered academic grades and pre-board grades per cluster on Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination performance and licensure success across batches.

## Specific Objectives
1. Describe the graduates' academic performance profile based on clustered grades.
2. Describe the graduates' pre-board examination performance per cluster.
3. Describe the graduates' RTLE outcomes in terms of board examination grades per cluster and pass/fail result.
4. Determine whether clustered academic grades are significantly related to RTLE performance.
5. Determine whether pre-board grades per cluster are significantly related to RTLE performance.
6. Determine whether clustered academic grades and pre-board grades per cluster significantly predict RTLE performance.
7. Determine whether clustered academic grades and pre-board grades per cluster significantly predict licensure success (pass/fail).
8. Identify which predictor or predictor set best explains variation in RTLE performance and licensure success.
9. Determine whether pre-board grades per cluster add predictive value beyond clustered academic grades in explaining RTLE performance and pass/fail outcome.
10. Compare academic performance, pre-board performance, RTLE performance, and licensure success across batches.

## Study Design
Retrospective descriptive-correlational and predictive research design.

## Population
Graduates of the Bachelor of Science in Radiologic Technology program who have taken the Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination.

## Setting
Philippines. Specific institution, academic years, and licensure examination periods: To be confirmed.

## Data Sources
- Academic records
- Clustered academic grades
- Pre-board grades per cluster
- Internship or clinical performance grades, if available
- Terminal competency or comprehensive examination results, if available
- Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination board examination grades per cluster
- Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination pass/fail outcomes
- Demographic or profile variables, if available
- Batch or cohort identifier

## Intended Analysis
- Descriptive statistics for clustered academic grades, pre-board grades per cluster, RTLE board examination grades per cluster, pass/fail outcomes, and batch profiles
- Correlation analysis between clustered academic grades, pre-board grades per cluster, and RTLE performance
- Multiple linear regression or comparable modeling for RTLE board examination grades per cluster or overall RTLE rating if available
- Logistic regression or comparable classification modeling for licensure success (pass/fail)
- Incremental predictive-value analysis to determine whether pre-board grades per cluster add explanatory value beyond clustered academic grades, if sample size and data quality allow
- Batch comparison analysis for academic performance, pre-board performance, RTLE performance, and licensure success

## Scope Boundary
The study should focus on institution-level predictive validity of clustered academic grades and pre-board grades per cluster for RTLE performance and licensure success across batches among Radiologic Technology graduates. It should not attempt to evaluate the entire curriculum, compare institutions broadly, evaluate teaching effectiveness, or infer causality unless the study design and data support such claims. Practical recommendations should be framed as screening, forecasting, early warning, and remediation-planning uses, not as guarantees of passing.
