# Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

## Inclusion Criteria for Study Participants
- Graduates of the Bachelor of Science in Radiologic Technology program.
- Graduates who took the Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination.
- Graduates with available academic records sufficient to compute clustered academic grades.
- Graduates with available licensure examination outcomes.
- Graduates with available pre-board grades per cluster, if pre-board performance is required in the final predictive model.
- Graduates with identifiable batch/cohort information for across-batch comparison.
- Cohort years: To be confirmed.
- Institution or program site: To be confirmed.

## Exclusion Criteria for Study Participants
- Students who did not graduate from the Bachelor of Science in Radiologic Technology program.
- Graduates who have not taken the Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination.
- Graduates with incomplete or unverifiable licensure examination outcomes.
- Records with missing key clustered academic grade, pre-board cluster grade, batch, or RTLE outcome variables that cannot be addressed through an appropriate missing-data procedure.
- Retakers, if the study is limited to first-time licensure examinees. This decision is to be confirmed.
- Transferees or students with incomplete institutional academic records, if their records prevent valid calculation of academic performance. This decision is to be confirmed.

## Inclusion Criteria for Literature Sources
- Peer-reviewed studies, theses, dissertations, institutional research reports, government documents, professional board documents, and credible academic sources.
- Studies on Radiologic Technology licensure examination performance, preferably from the Philippines.
- Studies examining academic performance, clustered grades, professional course grades, internship or clinical performance, comprehensive or terminal competency examinations, mock board examinations, pre-board examinations, pre-board cluster scores, board examination subject/cluster performance, or similar predictors of licensure performance.
- Allied health licensure studies only when Radiologic Technology-specific studies are insufficient.
- Sources relevant to predictive validity, academic preparedness, board examination readiness, curriculum alignment, or licensure examination preparation.
- Preferably open-access full-text sources.
- Publication date range: To be confirmed. A suggested range is the most recent 10 years, with older foundational sources allowed if highly relevant.

## Exclusion Criteria for Literature Sources
- Non-academic opinion pieces without empirical or theoretical value.
- Studies unrelated to health professions education or licensure assessment.
- Sources that discuss licensure performance without any relevance to predictors, preparation, assessment, or educational outcomes.
- Sources without sufficient methodological information, unless used only for background context.
- Paywalled sources when an equivalent open-access source is available.

## Screening Priorities
1. Philippine Radiologic Technology licensure studies.
2. Non-Philippine Radiologic Technology licensure or certification studies.
3. Philippine allied health licensure studies.
4. International allied health licensure studies.
5. General higher education predictive validity studies, only if needed for theoretical support.
