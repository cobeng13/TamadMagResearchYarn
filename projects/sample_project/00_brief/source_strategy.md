# Source Strategy

## Search Priorities
The literature search should prioritize sources in the following order:

1. Open-access Philippine studies on Radiologic Technology licensure examination performance.
2. Open-access Philippine studies on predictors of licensure examination performance in allied health programs.
3. International Radiologic Technology or radiography studies on certification, licensure, academic predictors, and clinical performance.
4. Health professions education studies on mock board examinations, comprehensive examinations, terminal competency assessments, and predictive validity.
5. Official sources from the Professional Regulation Commission, Commission on Higher Education, and institutional program documents for policy and context.

## Recommended Databases and Repositories
- Google Scholar
- PubMed
- ERIC
- DOAJ
- Philippine E-Journals
- HERDIN Plus
- institutional repositories of Philippine universities and colleges
- ResearchGate only as a discovery tool, followed by verification from the publisher or repository
- Professional Regulation Commission website
- Commission on Higher Education website

## Open-Access Strategy
- Start with open-access databases and institutional repositories.
- Use article title searches to locate repository copies of paywalled papers.
- Prefer publisher pages, university repositories, government documents, and indexed journals.
- Avoid relying on abstract-only records unless the source is used only for background context.

## Source Types Needed
- Empirical studies on predictors of licensure examination success.
- Radiologic Technology-specific studies on board performance, academic indicators, internship performance, and exit assessments.
- Allied health studies with comparable licensure structures.
- Methodological sources on retrospective correlational and predictive research designs.
- Sources on predictive validity and educational assessment.
- Policy or official documents on Radiologic Technology education and licensure in the Philippines.

## Review of Related Literature Themes
1. Radiologic Technology education and licensure in the Philippines.
2. Academic performance as a predictor of licensure examination performance.
3. Professional course grades and subject-specific preparation.
4. Internship, clinical performance, and practical competency indicators.
5. Pre-board and mock board examinations as readiness assessments.
6. Terminal competency and comprehensive examinations.
7. Curriculum alignment between program outcomes and licensure examination competencies.
8. Predictive modeling in health professions education.
9. Intervention implications for review programs, remediation, and student advising.

## Screening Guidance
- Record search string, database, search date, and number of results.
- Screen titles and abstracts first for relevance to licensure prediction.
- Prioritize full-text availability.
- Extract population, program, country, predictors, outcomes, statistical methods, and major conclusions.
- Distinguish Radiologic Technology evidence from allied health support literature in the synthesis.
- Do not overgeneralize allied health findings to Radiologic Technology without noting the disciplinary difference.

## Citation Management
- Use APA 7th edition.
- Store DOI, URL, database, and access date where relevant.
- Flag citations requiring verification.
- Ensure all sources cited in the manuscript appear in the reference list and all reference list entries are cited in text.
