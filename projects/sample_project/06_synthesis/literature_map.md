# Literature Map

## Current Study Focus

Predictive value of clustered academic grades and pre-board grades per cluster for Radiologic Technologist Licensure Examination (RTLE) performance and licensure success across BS Radiologic Technology batches in the Philippines (retrospective descriptive-correlational and predictive design).

## Evidence Clusters

- RT-specific studies showing pre-board/mock scores predict licensure or institutional outcomes (Rabinoa2025MockBoardExaminationResult, Ampaso2022).
- Allied-health and radiography multisite evidence supporting GWA/professional-course averages and standardized exit/pre-board exams as predictors, used cautiously to support cluster-based academic and pre-board operationalization (ChristineGouveia2024PredictiveValidityHesiRadiography, Flores2024, Wolden2019 meta-analysis).
- Philippine multi-program institutional predictive modeling illustrating methods and operationalization for professional-course averages and mock exams (Garcia2026).
- Institutional/aggregate analyses demonstrating cohort and institutional effects (Ibañez2025).
- Methodological literature on assessment construction and psychometrics relevant to pre-board instruments (Bera2024).
- Local RT descriptive/attitudinal studies and small-sample analyses highlighting measurement and sample-size issues (Oliva2021, Gallardo2025, Cabatingan2024).

## Directly Relevant Studies

- Rabinoa2025MockBoardExaminationResult (mock board → RTLE logistic regression; Philippine RT sample)
- Ampaso2022 (monthly exams → revalidation exam strong correlation; Philippine RT interns)
- Oliva2021 (RT study habits → GWA; operationalization of GWA in RT)
- Cabatingan2024 (admission test stanine vs licensure average; Philippine BSRT cohort)
- Ibañez2025 (institution-level counts and predictors across Philippine RT schools)

## Adjacent Allied Health / Education Evidence

- ChristineGouveia2024PredictiveValidityHesiRadiography (HESI Radiography exit exam predicting ARRT; U.S. radiography multi-program evidence)
- Flores2024 (Dentistry — GWA predicts licensure; pre-board not predictive in single-site sample)
- Wolden2019 (PT systematic review/meta-analysis showing GPA and comprehensive exams predict licensure; clinical performance weak)
- Garcia2026 (large Philippine multi-program DFA showing mock and professional knowledge strong discriminators)

## Methodological Evidence

- Bera2024 (item construction, correction-for-guessing, reliability/reproducibility checks)
- ChristineGouveia2024PredictiveValidityHesiRadiography (multiple operationalizations of pre-board scores, sensitivity analyses, path analysis for program policy mediation)
- Ibañez2025 (use of Poisson/negative binomial for count outcomes and overdispersion considerations)
- Garcia2026 (DFA with cross-validation example and operational definitions for predictors)

## Gaps Identified From Literature

- For the current study objectives, the key local gap is whether clustered academic grades and pre-board grades per cluster explain RTLE board examination grades per cluster and pass/fail licensure success across batches.

- Lack of large, multi-institutional, individual-level Philippine RT datasets linking GWA, professional-course grades, clinical/internship ratings, pre-board scores, and RTLE subject scores—most RT evidence is single-site or review-center specific.
- Inconsistent operationalization and limited psychometric reporting for institutional pre-board/mock exams (alignment with RTLE To be confirmed).
- Frequent small sample sizes (n<50) in RT-specific studies limiting statistical power and generalizability.
- Insufficient reporting on inclusion/exclusion of repeat exam takers and handling of missing data across cohort years in several institutional studies (To be confirmed).
- Limited evaluated interventions: proposed remediation/enhancement programs often untested or unevaluated.

## Contradictions or Mixed Findings

- Pre-board predictive value: positive findings in Rabinoa2025MockBoardExaminationResult, ChristineGouveia2024PredictiveValidityHesiRadiography, Garcia2026 vs null pre-board→licensure finding in Flores2024 (dentistry) and null case-study→revalidation in Ampaso2022 — suggests heterogeneity by instrument content, timing, and sample.
- Clinical/practicum predictive value: small/weak effect sizes in PT meta-analysis and limited discrimination in Garcia2026 vs assumed importance of clinical competence in professional practice—measurement differences likely explain disparity.

## How This Supports the Next Stage

The collected evidence supports designing the current retrospective predictive RT study to include: (1) individual-level predictors—GWA and professional-course averages, pre-board/mock exam scores (raw and adjusted), internship/practicum ratings; (2) covariates—cohort year, first-time vs repeater status, time since graduation, review participation; (3) psychometric checks on pre-board instruments (item-level difficulty, reliability, correction-for-guessing); (4) analytic strategies—correlation, logistic regression (pass/fail), linear regression (continuous licensure ratings if available), discriminant analysis or cross-validated classification, and sensitivity analyses for different operationalizations of pre-board scores. Also plan missing-data and cohort-year handling (e.g., COVID-19 disruptions).
